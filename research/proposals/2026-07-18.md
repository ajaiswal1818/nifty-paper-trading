## 3. Finding #1 — entry-fill modeling defect (systematic, platform-level)

The Jul 17 trade exposed a structural live-vs-backtest divergence:

- The morning run executed the "9:15 open" fill at 08:55 using the GIFT-implied spot
  **24,066**. The actual NSE open was **24,163** (+0.37% gap up, 97 pts away).
- Consequences, all on the same trade: (a) ATM strike selected as 24050 instead of 24150;
  (b) entry premium 116.65 for a put worth ~**76** the moment trading started — the −35% stop
  was fully consumed at t=0, and the monitor's 09:22 "stop" merely recorded a loss locked in
  at entry; (c) the gap-chase filter was evaluated on the GIFT gap (−0.03%) instead of the real
  gap (+0.37%). The backtest, by contrast, always fills at the session's true open — so live
  results will systematically diverge from the backtested strategy on any morning where GIFT
  parts from the actual open.
- Honesty note: this defect did **not** change the trade's sign or (materially) its size. The
  signal was wrong; a corrected fill (24150 PE @120.8 at open 24163) still loses ≈₹3.5k to the
  intraday −35% stop (or −₹5,377 under close-only checks). What the defect breaks is the
  fidelity of the A/B and of every future stop/target distance.
- **Process proposal (for the human; touches RUNBOOK/monitor design, no strategy params):**
  the morning run should record the entry *decision* pre-open, and the *fill* should be priced
  at the first real post-open quote — e.g. the monitor's first pass at/after 09:15 executes the
  queued entry at live spot, or the morning run is shifted to ~09:16 IST. Until then, every
  live entry embeds a random ±(GIFT-vs-open) error that the backtest never sees. This is the
  platform's most important open defect now that the evening-run timing is fixed.
